This paper proposes a cooperative longitudinal driving and lane assignment strategy for left-turn connected and autonomous vehicles (CAVs) at signalized intersections with a contraflow left-turn (CLT) lane. A mixed-integer linear optimization model is developed to jointly optimize longitudinal driving behavior and lane choices for left-turn vehicles while considering vehicle physical constraints and operational rules of CLT intersections.

To support real-time implementation, the paper further develops a computationally efficient solution algorithm based on Lagrange relaxation. Numerical results show that the proposed strategy outperforms noncooperative control based on IDM, reducing average delay by 24.66% and increasing throughput by 20.44%, thereby improving both safety and operational efficiency at intersections with a CLT lane.
